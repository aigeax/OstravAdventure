Komenského sady is a beautiful park alongside the Ostravice River. We visited one of the most beautiful parks in Ostrava, Komenského sady.

Ostravice River


Komenského sady is a beautiful park alongside the Ostravice River. We can find some structures from the past such as the red army statue and Busta Jana Amose Komenského. The river begins with the Ostravice after the confluence of the Bílá Ostravice and the Černá Ostravice. These are two streams flowing through deeply wooded valleys that are important access routes to the seaside resorts of Bílá and Bílý Kříž. Ostravace then creates a reservoir of fresh water behind the Šance dam, for the industrial region around Ostrava completed in 1970. It has an area of ​​335 km² and a height of 65 m and a length of 342 m.

The Red Army Statue of Ostrava


The Red Army Statue is a carefully guarded monument to the heroes who liberated their city as a strategic point of the so-called Third Reich, during the largest military operation in the territory of the former Czechoslovakia, at immeasurable human and technical losses. I saw a sad history about this monument, The Germans wanted to shoot a boy, then fifteen years old, in front of his father, for throwing food over the fence to captured Soviet soldiers. Honor and eternal memory to all the Red Army men and residents of Ostrava, who despite all the dangers helped them to liberate our homeland. And most of the Red Army men who have a memorial here were not even twenty years old. it's now beautifully illuminated, and the monument has thus become one of Ostrava's landmarks. A beautiful place in the middle of their garden, Komenského sady.

the bust of Jan Amos Comenius


For the bust of Jan Amos Comenius is located outdoors in the park in front of the Ostrava-Porub Primary School, Comenius in Ostrava-Porub in the Moravian-Silesian Region. The author of the work is the academic sculptor Vladislav Gajda (1925-2010).

I advise you to go there if you ever come to Ostrava, it's worth the trip!
