Ostrava City Center:

Ostrava is a city located in the east of the Czech Republic, known for its rich cultural and historical heritage. The center of the city is home to some of the region's most iconic landmarks, which attract thousands of visitors from all over the world every year.

Center of the city Ostrava


One of the most important monuments in the city is the Cathedral of the Holy Trinity. Located in the historic center of Ostrava, this neo-Gothic cathedral was built in the 19th century and is considered one of the finest examples of religious architecture in the region. With its 73 meter high tower, it dominates the urban landscape and offers a breathtaking view of the city.

Cathedral of the Holy Trinity


Another important city landmark is Slezskoostravský Castle, also known as Moravská Ostrava Castle. Built in the 14th century, this medieval castle is a real architectural gem, which has survived many wars and battles over the centuries. Today it houses a local history museum, where visitors can learn about the fascinating history of the town and its surroundings.

Moravská Ostrava Castle


The center of the city is also home to several historic squares, such as Masaryk Square, which is one of the largest squares in the city. This picturesque square is lined with historic buildings, shops and restaurants, and is a popular place to relax and stroll. Nearby is the Old Town Square, which is one of the oldest squares in the city and also home to many historic buildings.

Finally, the center of the city is also known for its unique industrial architecture. The city of Ostrava has a long history of industrial production, and many industrial buildings have been converted into public and cultural spaces. One of the most iconic examples is the Bolt Tower observation tower, which was built on the site of a former coal mine. With its 78 meters height, this tower offers a panoramic view of the city and is a popular meeting place for locals and tourists.
